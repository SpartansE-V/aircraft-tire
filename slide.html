The problem

Tires are the highest-frequency consumable. And the least predictable.

Aircraft tires are among the most frequently replaced components in airline operations — yet replacement planning is still reactive.

Unexpected wear is expensive.

A tire that wears faster than expected means delays, Aircraft-on-Ground events, emergency logistics, and rising maintenance cost.

Forecasting is limited.

Current capability gives little predictive visibility into remaining tire life, so planning stays reactive and inventory stays inefficient.

Data sits unused.

Years of inspection records, defect logs, and operational data exist — but are not leveraged for forecasting.

Proposed solution

See the tire in 3D. Predict its life. Decide with confidence.

Five use cases in one Tire Ops web app: computer-vision inspection rebuilt in 3D, a VLM that reads damage like an engineer, time-series RUL prediction from sensor data, next-flight wear simulation, and an agentic assistant that ties it together.

Flagship capability — UC2

3D damage inspection.

Multi-angle photos are fitted to a fixed tire mesh to rebuild each tire in 3D. Segmentation measures cracks and groove depth as geometry — not guesswork — and a VLM writes a severity report grounded in the manuals.

Computer vision 3D reconstruction VLM assessment

UC1 · Dashboard

Fleet tire dashboard.

Fleet status, RUL ranking, damage alerts, and the MEL watch-list — the whole fleet on one screen, worst wheels first.

UC3 · Simulation

Next-flight simulation.

Route, load, and runway in — projected post-flight wear out. A physics + ML model checks safety before dispatch.

UC4 · Prediction

Remaining-life prediction.

Usage, defects, and sensor parameters feed a time-series RUL model: cycles left ± CI. A decision agent recommends fly / monitor / replace.

UC5 · Assistant

Engineer chatbot.

An agent with tools — KB search, SQL records, RUL API, Sim API — answers engineers with citations and cuts lookup work.

Why 3D + VLM matters: geometric crack and groove-depth measurement makes both the human assessment and the downstream RUL prediction materially more accurate.
